Emergency Blood Request

Submit an emergency blood request. For life-threatening situations, call emergency services immediately.

Emergency Steps

  1. Call emergency services (911 / local emergency number) if life-threatening.
  2. Contact the nearest hospital blood bank immediately.
  3. Provide patient blood group, units needed, and location.
  4. Reach out to registered blood donation organizations.
  5. Use social media and community networks responsibly to find donors.

Donation Guide

Understanding the complete blood donation process

1

Registration

Provide identification and complete a registration form at the donation center.

2

Screening

Health questionnaire, mini physical exam, hemoglobin check, and blood pressure measurement.

3

Donation

Whole blood donation takes about 10–15 minutes. A sterile needle collects approximately 450ml.

4

Recovery

Rest for 10–15 minutes, enjoy refreshments, and avoid strenuous activity for the rest of the day.

5

Aftercare

Stay hydrated, eat iron-rich foods, keep the bandage on for several hours, and report any concerns.

Eligibility Check

General eligibility requirements for blood donation

Age Requirements

  • Minimum age: 17 years (16 with parental consent in some regions)
  • Maximum age: No upper limit if healthy (varies by country)
  • First-time donors: Must meet minimum age requirement

Weight Requirements

  • Minimum weight: 50 kg (110 lbs) typically required
  • Must be in good general health
  • Body weight ensures safe donation volume

Health Requirements

  • Normal hemoglobin levels (12.5 g/dL for women, 13.0 g/dL for men)
  • Normal blood pressure and pulse
  • No active infections or fever
  • Feeling well on donation day

Waiting Periods

  • Whole blood: 56 days between donations
  • Platelets: 7 days (up to 24 times/year)
  • After surgery: varies (consult blood bank)
  • After tattoos/piercings: 3–12 months (varies)

Common Restrictions

  • Recent travel to malaria-endemic areas
  • Certain medications (antibiotics, blood thinners)
  • Pregnancy and recent childbirth
  • High-risk behaviors (as per screening guidelines)

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about blood donation

Is blood donation safe?

Yes. Sterile, single-use equipment is used for every donor. You cannot get diseases from donating blood.

How often can I donate blood?

Whole blood can be donated every 56 days. Platelets can be donated more frequently — every 7 days up to 24 times per year.

Does donating blood weaken my immune system?

No. Your body replenishes donated blood within a few weeks. Donating does not weaken your immune system.

Which blood type is the universal donor?

O- is the universal donor for red blood cells. AB+ is the universal recipient.

What should I eat before donating?

Eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of water. Avoid fatty foods before donation as they can affect test results.

Can I donate if I have a tattoo?

Most centers require a waiting period of 3–12 months after getting a tattoo or piercing, depending on local regulations.
